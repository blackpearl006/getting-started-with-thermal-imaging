Study Overview

The ENIGMA-Parkinson's study is a large-scale international collaboration examining structural brain changes in Parkinson's disease. This dataset includes high-resolution T1-weighted MRI scans and diffusion tensor imaging from 342 participants across 12 sites. The study aims to identify reproducible patterns of gray matter atrophy and white matter changes associated with PD progression and cognitive decline.

Study Overview

AD-Connect is a comprehensive multimodal study investigating biomarkers of Alzheimer's disease progression. The dataset includes amyloid and tau PET imaging, structural MRI, comprehensive neuropsychological testing, CSF biomarkers, and plasma p-tau from 512 participants spanning the AD continuum from cognitively normal to mild dementia.

Study Overview

InfantBrain is an ongoing longitudinal study examining typical and atypical brain development from birth through 24 months. The study employs natural sleep MRI protocols optimized for infant imaging to capture developmental trajectories of brain structure, connectivity, and myelination during critical periods of early neurodevelopment. Study Overview

PD-Longitudinal is a prospective study tracking disease progression in early Parkinson's disease through annual multimodal assessments including structural MRI, dopamine transporter PET, comprehensive motor and cognitive testing, and wearable sensor monitoring. The study aims to identify imaging and digital biomarkers predictive of motor and cognitive decline trajectories, with particular focus on subtypes and progression heterogeneity. Current enrollment: 210 of 300 target participants.
