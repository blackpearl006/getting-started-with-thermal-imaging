Important Notes for Researchers

License & Access: Always verify licenses and usage rights for each dataset. Some datasets are fully open, while others may require permission or institutional access requests.

Dataset Focus: Datasets vary significantly in their focus:

  • Medical-Breast: Specifically for breast cancer thermography research with clinical pathology data
  • Medical-Other Body Parts: Limited availability; consider datasets for osteoarthritis, inflammation, or specific body-part pathology
  • General Human Thermal: Primarily focused on pose detection, human detection, or surveillance rather than medical pathology

Metadata Quality: High-quality datasets provide detailed metadata including:

  • Age, pathology status, number of subjects
  • Camera specifications and environmental conditions
  • Capture protocol and standardization details

Recommended Categorization: When working with thermal imaging data, consider splitting your research into:

  • Medical Thermography: Clinical applications with pathology focus
  • Non-Medical Thermal Imaging: Pose estimation, human detection, and computer vision applications

Additional Resources: For medical body-part thermal imaging beyond breast (e.g., limbs, arthritis, inflammation), additional database searches may be required as public availability is more limited.
